Bold space names — less trodden

Same register as Apogee and Telemetry, easier to say, rarely used in software — click any you like

Parallax

PAR-uh-lax · 3 syllables · navigation concept

The apparent shift in position of a star when viewed from different angles — how astronomers measure distance. Fits a tool that gives you a complete view of your library from every angle. Barely used as a product name.

Azimuth

AZ-ih-muth · cockpit instrument · almost never used

The horizontal angle used to track objects across the sky — you'd find it on a telescope mount or a radar dish. Pure mission-control vocabulary. Distinctive, confident, and essentially untaken in the software world.

Zenith

ZEE-nith · 2 syllables · clean and immediate

The point directly overhead — the highest point in the sky from where you're standing. Easier than Apogee, same confident feel. Signals that this is the definitive tool for the job. Some minor software use but nothing dominant.

Antares

an-TAR-eez · red supergiant star · bold

One of the largest and brightest stars in the sky, 700 times the size of the Sun. Also the name of a NASA cargo rocket. Strong, distinctive, sounds like serious software without being generic. Minimal conflicts in the product world.

Umbra

UM-bruh · 2 syllables · sleek and precise

The darkest, most precise region of a shadow during an eclipse — where everything extraneous is cut away and only the essential remains. Short, clean, slightly dramatic. The feel of precision tooling.
